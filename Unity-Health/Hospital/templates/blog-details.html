Weekly Covid cases up 5%; fall in Kerala, but rise in 2 states

Covid-19 cases in the country, currently on the upswing due to the spread of a new JN.1 subvariant, registered only a marginal 5% rise in the past week, with infections continuing to dip sharply in Kerala while increasing steadily in Karnataka and Maharashtra, among the major states. India reported 31 fatalities due to the virus, nearly the same as in the previous week (29). The country logged 4,862 fresh cases during the week (December 31-January 6) as compared to 4,652 in the previous seven days.

Health officials attribute the rise in Covid-19 cases to increased transmission rates associated with the JN.1 subvariant, which is believed to be more contagious but not necessarily more severe. States like Karnataka and Maharashtra are ramping up testing and vaccination efforts to curb the spread, while Kerala’s steady decline in cases reflects the success of targeted public health measures. The marginal increase in fatalities has sparked renewed emphasis on booster doses and adherence to safety protocols, particularly in crowded urban areas. Experts continue to monitor the situation closely, with genome sequencing underway to track the subvariant’s prevalence and potential mutations.
